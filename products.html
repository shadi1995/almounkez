Enterprise Resource Planning

ERP

Managing financial accounts:
organizing the accounts guide, financial entries, financial movement, account card, trial balance, cash flow operations, budget reports, and income statement

Warehouse Operations Management:
Organizing entry, exit and transfer of materials, managing daily operations, loading import operations with purchase expenses, loading selling operations with transportation and loading expenses, issuing daily movement reports of materials within warehouses, calculating the weighted cost of outputs, and issuing inventory lists for warehouses and materials.

Manufacturing operations management:
Organizing manufacturing processes, extracting raw materials and production, adding direct costs to production processes and distributing them to reach the direct cost of the product.

Human resource management system

HRMS

Employment requests:
Receiving, archiving, following up and transferring employment requests to the Responsibility Department upon employment.

Self-information:
All employee information, identification papers, certificates, and all private information such as vacations, rewards, penalties, courses, transfers, assignments.

Time monitoring:
Support for time monitoring devices in an integrated manner, including entry and exit processes, counting delays, overtime, and shift monitoring

Salaries and Wages:
Managing the processes of issuing monthly salary and wages tables and issuing bonuses, taking into account advances, taxes and fees.


Customer Relationship Management

CRM

  • Contact Information: Maintaining all customer data, their addresses, employee names, subsidiary phones, e-mail, geographical location, communication methods, and event cards.
  • Dealings: all the needs that require management to build the best relationships; Maintaining all operations that took place between the company and the client, such as meetings, correspondence, quotations, sales contracts, communications, delegate visits and their results.
  • Quotations integrated information on the quotations that the Company offers to customers, with their dates and results; It is allowed to remind them to follow them up and finish their implementation.
  • Follow-up sales plans Develop a sales plan for delegates, follow up on monthly sales for each representative, and compare the plan with actual sales.
